## Substantive Responses

Most of your inline responses are either things we agree on or where I accept your reasoning. I'll focus on the items where I have follow-up.

**Item 2 (payments — omit for Phase 1):** Agreed, with the caveat above — Phase 2 payment terms need to be in active development immediately, not queued for "a future phase." Trust-based per-connection pricing, the 100% satisfaction guarantee, pre-purchases, Evryn Credit, and Stripe Connect peer-to-peer payments each have their own legal considerations, as you noted. I'd rather that work start now so it's ready when Phase 2 launches.

**Item 4 (PII prohibition):** The existing carveout covers name, email, and phone number — but forwarded emails routinely contain professional history, project details, personal circumstances, and occasionally financial or health references in their body text. That's inherent to email content, and processing it is the core service. The carveout needs to cover email body content, not just contact fields. Otherwise a user forwarding a typical inbound email is technically violating the Terms.

**Item 5 (no guarantee of matches, right to stop matching):** I understand the Phase 1 scope concern, but this applies *now*, not just in Phase 2. Even in Phase 1, Mark could take issue with Evryn missing someone in his inbound, or we could encounter a situation where we need to decline service. "No guarantee of surfacing any particular contact" and "Evryn may discontinue service to users who behave in harmful ways" are protective provisions we need from day one — they don't require matching functionality to be relevant.

**Item 8 ("no ownership" clarification):** This was described in detail in several of the materials I sent:

- **Questionnaire** (Additional Context, "Mutual matching"): *"Everyone who comes through a gatekeeper's inbound becomes a full Evryn user... The same person may have reached out to multiple gatekeepers simultaneously, but each gatekeeper will naturally assume these are 'their leads.' They are not — we'd want the Terms to be clear about this and disclaim any ownership or priority rights over the people in a gatekeeper's inbound."*
- **Gatekeeper Flow doc** (Phase II, "Everyone Becomes an Evryn User"): *"The gatekeeper should assume no ownership or exclusive rights over people who come through their inbound."*

In short: when Mark forwards emails to Evryn, the people in those emails become Evryn users. Mark has no ownership, priority, or exclusive rights over them. Evryn's obligation is to each user individually. This is the same principle as your "no-priority concept" — I think we're saying the same thing with different words.

**Item 11 (safety identifier):** I accept the advice to keep detailed security practice descriptions out of the Privacy Policy. A trust center or FAQ is fine for the implementation details. But I do want the Privacy Policy to disclose the *fact* that a non-reversible identifier is retained after account deletion, even if it doesn't describe the cryptographic implementation. Users should know this before they sign up, not discover it in an FAQ.

**Item 13 (automated decision-making):** Your legal reasoning on the GDPR Article 22 / state privacy law implications is helpful and I accept the recommendation to retain the current language for Phase 1. Agreed that this needs to be revisited as we scale and reduce human review.

**Item 14 (user record creation disclosure):** The proposed language — *"to analyze communication patterns and assist the Initiating User in managing their relationships"* — doesn't accurately describe what we do. Evryn doesn't help users "manage their relationships." She evaluates mutual fit between people and may, in Phase 2, reach out to those individuals directly as potential Evryn users in their own right. The disclosure should reflect that these individuals' information is being evaluated by an AI, and that they may be contacted in a future phase. Something more like: *"When an Initiating User forwards their incoming emails to Evryn, we collect and process personal information contained in those emails, including the sender's name, email address, message content, and metadata. We use this information to evaluate potential connections and may contact these individuals in the future to offer our services."*

As before — that's directional, not prescriptive. Write it however it needs to be written legally. But the current proposed language would be inaccurate.
