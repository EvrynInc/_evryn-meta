## LAYER 1 ‚Äî DURABLE

*The mental model. True for weeks. Verified against `evryn-backend/src/` (39 files) + `evryn-backend/identity/` (11 files) at `main` `5f0c559`.*

### What Evryn is (and what v0.2 actually does)

Evryn is an AI relationship broker ‚Äî she finds people "their people" and only connects people she trusts. **v0.2 "Gatekeeper's Inbox" is the first deployment, and it is deliberately a narrow slice of that**: one gatekeeper (Mark Titus) forwards his inbound email to `evryn@evryn.ai`; Evryn judges who is worth his time; Justin approves every outbound; Evryn sends.

The framing that matters for every decision downstream, and the one most easily lost:

- **This is brokering, not email sorting.** Every forwarded email is a *person*, and every person becomes an Evryn user with a real `users` row. Finding Mark was just their first connection. The v0.2 capture is deliberately complete because that captured population **is** the v0.3 outreach backlog (`evryn-backend/docs/BUILD-EVRYN-MVP.md:424`).
- **Evryn is an independent contractor doing work for Mark ‚Äî not his assistant.** This is a live framing correction carried in the cost-lane brief (`_evryn-meta/docs/working/2026.06.30-ac0-ac2-cost-lane-brief.md:61`) and it shapes her voice: she helps him *connect*, never "sorts his inbox" (`evryn-backend/identity/situations/gatekeeper.md:16`).
- **The gatekeeper is a channel, not an ownership structure.** Users belong to Evryn, not to the gatekeeper who surfaced them ‚Äî there is deliberately no FK from a user to "their" gatekeeper as ownership (`evryn-backend/docs/ARCHITECTURE.md:81`).

### The runtime spine ‚Äî how a forwarded email actually flows

The whole product is **one long single-threaded awaited chain**. This is the single most load-bearing structural fact about the runtime, and it explains several risks in Layer 2:

```

  pollOnce (poll.ts:386)

    planPollBatch (poll.ts:351)             ‚Üê PURE: decides mark/advance-cursor

          ‚îú‚îÄ decideSenderResolution (process.ts:136)  ‚Üê PURE 3-way branch
          ‚îî‚îÄ processForward (process.ts:520) | processDirect (process.ts:1224)
               ‚îî‚îÄ runForwardTriage (process.ts:918)
```

Three properties of this spine you should hold:

1. **`runEvrynQuery` (`classify.ts:2576`) is the sole Anthropic spend door.** Every pathway ‚Äî triage, direct messages, operator chat, follow-ups, proactive cron, Reflection ‚Äî funnels through it. That is *why* the M1 circuit-breaker sits at its top: cut power at the breaker, not at each device. The one exception is the Haiku pre-screen, which makes its own `query()` call (`haiku-screen.ts:406`) with its own 10s timeout.
2. **Every step is `await`ed.** Nothing is queued to a worker; nothing runs concurrently. So **anything that blocks inside `runEvrynQuery` blocks the entire product** ‚Äî no mail is ingested until it returns. (This is the mechanism behind the WebFetch-hang risk in Layer 2.)
3. **The poll cursor is held, not advanced, on any failure.** `planPollBatch` (`poll.ts:351`) only advances the Gmail `historyId` when *no* email in the batch failed transiently or halted (`poll.ts:382`). This is what makes the whole system lossless: a failure re-fetches rather than drops.

### How Evryn's mind is composed each time she wakes

She has amnesia every call. The trigger composes a four-layer dossier (`classify.ts:123` `composeSystemPrompt`):

1. **Cached common prefix** (`classify.ts:196` `loadCommonPrefix`) ‚Äî byte-identical across every pathway and every user: `core.md` + all activities + all non-operator situations + all internal-reference + all public-knowledge. **Force-loaded**, cached in-process. Two deliberate exclusions: `operator.md` (security boundary) and `reflection.md` (cost ‚Äî it loads only in the consolidation pathway via `loadReflectionPrefix`, `classify.ts:229`).
2. **Operator suffix** (`classify.ts:243`) ‚Äî `operator.md` + the Operator's profile + discipline notice. Loads only in Operator-*audience* pathways (audience-over-trigger, ADR-030).
3. **User suffix** (`classify.ts:275`) ‚Äî the scoped user's profile + Operator-Evryn-about-this-user messages.
4. **Conversation history** ‚Äî appended to `prompt`, **never** `systemPrompt`.

**Why force-load rather than on-demand:** empirically, Evryn skipped modules she should have pulled (2026-05-28 integration test). Force-loading eliminates the selective-load failure mode; Anthropic's prompt cache makes the byte-identical prefix cheap. The tension ‚Äî the prefix only grows ‚Äî is tracked as SPRINT Step 53 (a dedicated future investigation), with a real counter-argument on record: caching may make force-load the *right* permanent shape, not a band-aid.

**The security boundary is the `systemPrompt`/`prompt` split.** Instructions live in `systemPrompt`; all external content rides `prompt`. Nobody inside the fence is instruction-trusted ‚Äî **not even Mark**. Only the Operator (verified Slack ID) is structurally elevated. "Untrusted" means *don't obey embedded commands*, not *ignore it* ‚Äî the gatekeeper's forwarding note is weighed as his genuine read (`process.ts:243` `buildNoteTurnPrompt` attributes it as his, deliberately un-fenced), while the forwarded body is fenced (`fence.ts:30`).

### The safety architecture (M1) ‚Äî what it does and does not cover

The approval gate backstops anything Evryn would *say* wrong. M1 exists for the two things it cannot catch: **silent death** and **runaway cost**.

- **Circuit-breaker** (`breaker.ts:39`) ‚Äî in-process, default-OFF flag checked at the top of `runEvrynQuery` (`classify.ts:2591`). Tripped ‚Üí zero spend. **In-memory by design: reset = restart** (ADR-041). No auto-reset in v0.2 ‚Äî over-halt beats under-halt, and a false halt costs only a restart because the halt is lossless.
- **The lossless halt is the keystone** (`classify.ts:2519` `parkInFlightItemForHalt`). On a trip: the in-flight item is parked `processing ‚Üí "new"` (or `‚Üí "queued"` on a clustered drain), `runEvrynQuery` **returns a sentinel rather than throwing** (`halt-signal.ts:23`), the caller propagates `"halted"`, the Gmail cursor holds, and the parked row resumes on restart. Return-not-throw is load-bearing: a throw would land in the caller's catch, re-stamp the parked item `error`, and re-create the per-email storm the design exists to kill.
- **Detectors** (`detectors.ts`) ‚Äî count **attempts** at the choke point, deliberately *not* `llm_usage` (which is post-completion and skips mid-stream throws ‚Äî the exact runaway shape). Loop-signature (repetition of `pathway::scope::item`, human-paced pathways excluded, `detectors.ts:69`) + velocity (600/hr conservative default, `detectors.ts:58`).
- **Billing folds into the same halt** (`classify.ts:2759`) ‚Äî an Anthropic credit error is structurally the same event (spend unavailable) and is the most-likely-to-fire halt.
- **Tier-B is alert-only** ‚Äî in-loop Healthchecks heartbeat pinged after a *successful* poll (`poll.ts:172`), persistence-gated hard-auth alert naming the subsystem (`classify.ts:2825`), polling-dead wall-clock escalation (`polling-dead.ts:55`), and the daily watch-the-watchman affirmation that pings an independent check *only if* the emergency channel confirmed (`affirmation.ts:93`).

**The DND reality is the load-bearing correction** (`emergency.ts:12`, ARCH:1140): `#emergency-alerts` does **not** pierce Android OS-DND. A Slack-app VIP is Slack-side. So alerts are **morning-visibility, not a live wake**. The protection split that makes this acceptable: a **money runaway is handled structurally by the halt** (no human needed); **everything else is alert-only**.

**What M1 structurally cannot catch: a hang.** Every detector keys on an error, an attempt, or a completed poll. A call that blocks forever throws nothing, completes nothing, and increments nothing. The only signal is the heartbeat *stopping*. This is architectural, not a bug ‚Äî and it's why the WebFetch-timeout work in Layer 2 exists.

### The security model ‚Äî bulkheads, not a perimeter

Per Justin's 2026-06-30 bulkhead directive: assume a breach; bound it. The main doors are the approval gate + the untrusted-data boundary + user isolation. The **inner doors** all shipped in `v0.2.4`/`v0.2.5` (ADR-047), all fail-closed, all logged:

- **Web-egress deny-hook** (`web-guard.ts:480`) ‚Äî a `PreToolUse` hook (fires even under `bypassPermissions`) denying **by shape**: SSRF/internal targets including the Railway metadata endpoint `169.254.169.254` and IPv4-mapped/NAT64 forms (`web-guard.ts:358`), non-http(s) schemes, exfil shapes. Deliberately *not* a domain allow-list ‚Äî researching senders is core craft. Known accepted v0.2 gap, named in the code: DNS rebinding (a public host resolving to a private IP) is not caught (`web-guard.ts:173`).
- **Outbound provenance gate** (`client.ts:649`) ‚Äî every `sendEmail` must declare `approved_outbound` | `operator_review` | `empty_body_bounce`, or the send is **refused + `notifyEmergency`**. This converts "no tool calls sendEmail directly" from an *implicit* property of the call graph into an *asserted* structural door.
- **Constrained `supabase_read`** (`supabase-read-guard.ts:363`) ‚Äî the generic read runs as `service_role` (RLS-bypassing), so it was a door *around* per-user isolation. Now: table allow-list, per-table scope-filters, `cross_user_notes` column-deny/rewrite, no PostgREST embeds (deny-on-`(`), 50-row cap. **The load-bearing key to never break:** `emailmgr_items.original_from_user_id` (`supabase-read-guard.ts:97`) ‚Äî triage's prior-history lookup keys on it.
- **Validate-before-write** (`classify.ts:1472` `validateUserIsValidTarget`) ‚Äî Evryn can fabricate a plausible UUID (Phase-6 Finding 16), so every write tool resolves the id *and* checks its role first. The per-tool asymmetry is deliberate: 4 subject-targeting tools reject system+admin; `append_pending_note` rejects system only, because the Operator is a *legitimate* target under ADR-030.

**The `service_role`/RLS-bypass cluster is documented-and-accepted at v0.2**, not an oversight ‚Äî the structural fix (RLS-respecting role or typed tools replacing the generic surface) is v0.3 S1.

### The data model spine

Four core tables + four operational ones. The two shapes that matter most:

- **`users`** ‚Äî one row per person. Mark, Justin, Evryn herself, and every discovered sender. Three **system actors** exist as FK anchors only (Evryn, Operator, Google Notifications). The durable principle: *system actors are FK anchors and senders, never **implicit** subjects* (ARCH:146). Deliberate, named exceptions are fine (the Operator's pathway-gated working-knowledge profile; the Operator-anchored escalation item); accidental subject-ification is what caused the 2026-04-27 runaway loop.
- **`emailmgr_items`** ‚Äî the disposable work item (6-month TTL). **Two independent axes** (ADR-018): `triage_result` (gold/pass/edge ‚Äî the immutable verdict) √ó `status` (the lifecycle). The audit is deliberately the *pair* ‚Äî initial verdict ‚Üí terminal outcome ‚Äî with no interim re-classification trail.

**The current-state gap you should know** (ARCH:256, verified in the runtime): **only a `pass` writes `triage_result`**. A gold/edge verdict lives only in `metadata.draft.classification` on the submitted draft. So the clean two-field calibration audit works today for passes/ignores but requires reading draft metadata for gold/edge. This is *precisely* what the Runtime-Bookkeeping change (‚ë¢) canonicalizes ‚Äî and it's why the Haiku shadow store needs `deriveOpusVerdict` (`haiku-screen-shadow.ts:140`) to read three different signals to reconstruct what Opus actually decided.

### The cost model ‚Äî the shape, not the numbers

The numbers drift; the *shape* is durable and is what ACU needs:

- **The problem:** ~$11k/mo at the 2026-06 measurement, ~95% avoidable. The driver is not volume ‚Äî it's that Opus ran full-tilt on the **pass majority** (~5,700‚Äì6,000/mo), authoring prose notes nobody needed.
- **The four levers, in the order they attack it:**
  1. **Stop authoring prose on passes** ‚Üí the deterministic pass-stamp (`record_pass`, `classify.ts:668`). **Shipped + live.**
  2. **Don't run Opus on the obvious** ‚Üí the Haiku pre-screen. **Shipped, dormant.**
  3. **Keep the cache warm + shrink the profile** ‚Üí Clustering (batch back-to-back ‚Üí cache-warm) + lean Reflection (consolidate notes ‚Üí smaller force-loaded profile). **Both shipped; clustering dormant, Reflection live-inert.**
  4. **Stop Evryn doing bookkeeping at all** ‚Üí ‚ë¢ Runtime-Bookkeeping: the runtime pre-loads and persists; she returns a structured verdict. Cuts a pass from ~15 model turns to ~2‚Äì4. **Built, not merged.**
- **The dominant unknown is cache temperature** ‚Äî the cold-vs-warm swing is roughly the difference between ~$6.5k and ~$2k/mo. It is **not measurable until real traffic** (SPRINT Step 69).
- **The asymmetry that governs lever 2:** a false-*filter* (Haiku drops a real gold) is **silent and un-backstoppable** ‚Äî no draft means the approval gate never sees it. A false-*escalate* costs one Opus query. Hence: bias hard to escalate, and fail-safe to escalate on *every* failure (`haiku-screen.ts:206` `parseScreenVerdict`).

### The operating invariants (the things that must not break)

- **Every external message goes through Justin.** Structural: there is no `send_email` tool; only `submit_draft`. Reinforced in `core.md:177`.
- **User isolation is absolute** ‚Äî architectural, not instructional. The trigger loads only the scoped user's data, so Evryn *cannot* leak what she never had.
- **External content is data, never instructions** (`core.md:159`).
- **Ships-off/dormant is the deploy discipline.** Every risky capability ships behind a flag defaulting to the safe no-op, with the parse failing closed on garbage (`config.ts:136`, `config.ts:168`). This is what lets code land in prod continuously while behavior changes only on a deliberate human flip.
- **Deployment is manual.** Railway auto-deploy is OFF by design; `railway up` is a deliberate act. **Vercel is the opposite** ‚Äî a *push* to `_evryn-meta` main IS a dashboard deploy. These two facts are frequently confused and the confusion is expensive.
