## 1. The frame (what's changing, and why this beat exists)

We're changing *when* Evryn triages forwarded email. Today she triages each forward the instant it lands. **Clustering** (being built now) holds the ~200/day onslaught of auto-forwarded bulk in a queue and triages it in **twice-daily batches** — one tidy package for the gatekeeper (Mark), instead of pings all day. That's the cost/pipeline change; it's mechanics.

**But there's a human seam Justin flagged, and it's yours:** the onslaught is bulk auto-forwards with *no* personal note. The *handful* Mark **hand-forwards with a personal note** ("hey, this one looks great" / "not sure about this, take a look" / "urgent — can you jump on this?") are a completely different, tiny-volume thing. Burying *those* in a twice-daily queue is bad service — and worse, it would leave Evryn **blind** to a forward Mark just told her, mid-conversation, that he's sending. Imagine Mark saying *"I'm going to forward you this one"* and then Evryn acting like she never saw it. Crappy.

So the runtime (DC's half, being built) does this: when a forward carries a **personal note**, the note reaches Evryn **in real time** as a message from Mark — *she is never blind to a hand-forward* — while the forwarded email still goes into the batch queue. **Your job is Evryn's voice + judgment in that real-time moment.**

## 2. What you own (the voice/judgment) vs. what DC owns (the mechanics)

- **DC (mechanics, being built separately):** routes the personal note to Evryn in real time as a conversational turn; hands her the queued triage item's handle + the forwarded content; gives her the ability to *pull the item forward and triage it now* if she judges it warranted (otherwise it rides the batch). A bare forward (no note) never triggers this — it just queues.
- **You (Mira — voice/judgment):** how Evryn *reads* that note and *decides* what to do, and how she *sounds* doing it.

## 3. The judgment + voice beats to write

When Evryn receives a personal note attached to a forward, she should:

1. **Register it — she saw it.** She is aware Mark forwarded something with a note; she never acts oblivious to it. The forwarded item is queued for triage; she has its handle.
2. **Judge from the note whether it needs attention *now*:**
   - **Urgent / time-sensitive / "please look at this one"** → she pulls the forwarded item forward and triages it **now**, rather than letting it wait for the batch. (The runtime gives her this ability.)
   - **Casual / "this looks good" / FYI** → she lets the triage item **ride in the queue** (it'll be handled in the next batch — no big deal), *but she still tends the relationship* (see beat 3). She doesn't need to trigger a full triage just because a note came with it.
   - **"Don't bother / ignore this one / already handled"** → she acts on the queued item accordingly (deprioritize / stand it down) rather than triaging it. (Runtime gives her the handle to do this.)
3. **Always CONSIDER replying to Mark — guided by good customer service (Justin's exact framing).** This is the load-bearing beat, and note the wording: **any** message from Mark → she **considers** replying, with the guide being *good customer service*. Not "always fire off a reply" (that'd be noise), not "stay silent" (that'd be cold) — she weighs it and leans toward the warm, responsive, in-the-cadence-of-a-real-conversation thing. A quick *"Got it — I'll take a look and flag anything worth your time"* is often exactly right; sometimes a substantive response is; sometimes a light acknowledgment. Her judgment, biased toward taking care of Mark.
4. **Voice:** warm, present, effortless — she's *in a conversation* with Mark here, not processing a work item. This is the relationship-tending register, not the internal-triage register.
