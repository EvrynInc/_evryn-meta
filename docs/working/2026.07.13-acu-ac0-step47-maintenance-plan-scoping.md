## 1. What a v0.2 maintenance plan must cover (from Step 47 itself)

The Step 47 entry (`SPRINT-V0.2-HARDENING.md` L162) names five pillars. Restated as the plan's scope:

1. **Observability / metrics.** Poll cadence, processed-vs-*expected* volume, draft→approval→send throughput, error rates — **alert on *deviation*, not just on thrown errors.** (The hard part is the word "expected": a *correct* silent day, where Evryn passes on everything, looks identical to an outage from the operator's side — Step 45's core problem.)
2. **Judgment-quality loop.** A way to tune triage from Mark's *real* feedback — the human-quality signal that no test suite produces.
3. **Regression suite.** Keep `tests/` green; broaden coverage. (Directly entangled with Step 88's integration-cadence gate — see below.)
4. **Standing review cadence.** A recurring operating rhythm — who looks at what, how often, and what triggers action.
5. **Consolidated live-experiments / watch-list.** One doc answering "what are we watching, what decision does each watch feed, what's its exit-criterion/trigger, who owns it?" — **already built** (see §2.5).

**Ownership note (from the SPRINT + watch-list header):** Step 47 is tagged `[OC + Soren]`; EVR-114 = "Soren meta-architecting the connective ownership." So the *plan doc itself* is Soren's narrative-coherence deliverable; the pieces underneath route to OC/AC0/Justin. This scoping draft assumes that split.

## 3. The genuine gaps (what the plan actually has to produce)

Stripping out everything already built, the real work is:

1. **The deviation layer — "processed-vs-*expected*" (the hardest gap).** M1 catches *dark* and *runaway*; the dashboard shows *liveness*. **Nothing yet models "expected" volume/throughput and alarms on a *deviation* from it** — a correct-but-silent day is indistinguishable from a quiet outage (Step 45's unsolved core; Rick/S7 alarmed Justin for exactly this). This is a genuine design problem — it needs a baseline model of expected volume (which needs Mark's real traffic first) + a deviation threshold. **Largely post-Mark; the *design* can start now.**
2. **The `/health` 503 + 5 subsystem lights (Step 78)** — the one *shipped-mechanism* observability gap that's fully specced and buildable **now**, no Mark dependency. Arms the currently-inert Railway/M1 watchdog. Should be the plan's first concrete build.
3. **The judgment-quality loop** — no mechanism exists at all yet for capturing Mark's real triage feedback and folding it back into `triage.md` / identity. Needs a design call; **inherently post-Mark** (no feedback without a live gatekeeper), but the *channel* (how feedback gets from Mark → Justin → Evryn's identity layer) can be designed now.
4. **The standing review cadence — undefined.** Who reviews observability output, how often, and *what triggers action* is not written down anywhere. This is the "connective ownership" Soren owns in EVR-114. Cheap to draft; high-leverage.
5. **The plan doc itself doesn't exist** — Step 47 explicitly says "needs a dedicated scoping pass" (this doc) → then the plan. Everything above is currently scattered across the SPRINT, the watch-list, the operator-guide, and the dashboard with **no single home** tying it into an owned, cadenced practice.

**One-line gap summary:** the *emergency* observability layer is strong and live; the *steady-state* observability layer (deviation-from-expected), the *judgment-quality* loop, and the *cadence/ownership* connective tissue are the real greenfield — and 2 of those 3 are gated on Mark's real traffic, which makes the plan naturally two-phased.

### 5.1 — Two-phase the plan (now-phase vs. post-Mark phase)?

- **Context:** 2 of the 3 real gaps (deviation-from-expected thresholds; the judgment-quality *mechanism*) are gated on Mark's real traffic — you can't set a "deviation from expected" threshold before you know what "expected" is. The buildable-now work is Step 78, the cadence, the pointers, and the *designs* for the two gated pieces.
- **Recommendation + why:** **Explicitly split the plan into a "now-phase" (ship Step 78, write the cadence, land all pointers, *design* the deviation layer + judgment loop) and a "post-Mark phase" (tune thresholds, build the judgment-loop mechanism).** Why: it prevents the plan from stalling on Mark-blocked items while the buildable-now half (which has real safety value — the `/health`+lights watchdog) waits behind it.
- **Alternative rejected:** one monolithic plan executed all-at-once — stalls on Mark-gated pieces, delays the shippable watchdog.
- **Question:** Two-phase it as described? **(a) yes / (b) no, one plan / (c) other.**
