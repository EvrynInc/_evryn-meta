## 🔑 The structural lever — Justin's idea, and ACU's honest read

**Justin (2026-07-16):** *"We need to stop splitting off new ACs when there's something to do. That has me handling their shit all day, when, since we can do 'resume subagent' now, we could have all sub-ACs as sub-agents. All the more reason to make sure that the orchestration protocol robustly handles ACs. That would probably be the biggest possible lever on all of this."*

**He is right about the problem and it is the biggest lever available.** Today he babysits ~8 parallel top-level sessions (AC0, AC2, AC3, ACf, ACf1, ACf2, ACU, Lucas), and *this manifest's central finding is that his attention is the whole constraint*. Anything that cuts the number of surfaces claiming it beats anything that adds compute.

**But ACU does not think "make all sub-ACs subagents" is quite the right cut, and the difference matters:**

- **What's genuinely broken isn't that they're top-level — it's that each one treats Justin as its default question-sink.** The lanes that *should* be subagents (DC, QC, research, reporters) already are. The eight top-level ACs are top-level *precisely because* someone judged they need Justin's judgment.
- **Three real costs of a pure-subagent model:** (1) the conductor becomes a **lossy relay** — the AC manual's own warning is that subagents *"summarize destructively… strip nuance… return summaries that can actively mislead"*; (2) **Justin loses the direct channel**, and his manual says he is *"lethally sharp"* at catching the one thing an AC missed — which works because he's talking to the AC that holds the context, not to a summary of it; (3) **conductor context is finite** — holding six lanes of relay compacts the conductor, and a compacted conductor is the exact "soft" state that produces confident-wrong work.
- **A resume is not free** — it re-sends the whole transcript. A ~500K-token AC resumed ten times is ~5M tokens. Irrelevant against an unlimited budget; **not** irrelevant against a 5-hr window, which is our actual governor.

**⇒ ACU's recommendation: cut on *decision-density*, not on mechanism.**
- **The test for every lane:** *does this lane need Justin's judgment, or just his rubber stamp?* That is **precisely the question the two state reports now answer** — "what's gated on Justin vs. agent-autonomous" was one of the seven asks, and both reports answered it concretely.
- **Judgment-dense → stays top-level.** Rubber-stamp/execution-dense → **becomes a resumable subagent under a conductor.** ACU's read is that this genuinely collapses several of the eight — but which ones is a *finding from those reports*, not a guess to make now.
- **⚠️ The confound worth naming:** Justin put the lanes on Fable **specifically to make them more independent** — to answer their own questions and stop reaching for him. **Fable then bricked**, so today every lane is on Opus and *less* independent → **more Justin**. So some of today's "handling their shit all day" is a Fable-outage artifact, not a structural truth. **Don't restructure the org around a bad day.** Re-read the signal once Fable is back.

**Either way, the protocol work is the enabler and should land regardless** — see the open ask below. It costs ~10 minutes and it's a prerequisite to any version of this.
