## Beat 3 — `core.md`: the revision-path history double-attach (Sprint Step 67a) — **DESIGN CALL FIRST**

**The tension (real, pre-exists Lane B — surfaced by the foundation audit):**
- `core.md` (~line 240, "The runtime handles conversation history") tells Evryn: the runtime appends prior message history for you — do NOT include your own history/context block, or it duplicates.
- BUT `handleRevisionNotes` (`src/approval/flow.ts`, ~line 1032–1047) *feeds conversation history into Evryn's revision prompt* so she can revise with thread context (this was itself a 2026-06-06 QC-blocker fix). So on the revision path she *sees* history in her prompt, might echo it into the revised draft, and the runtime then appends history again at send → a possible double-attach.

**This needs a decision before any beat is written** (Justin + Mira, maybe Soren). Two clean directions:
- **(a)** the runtime *stops* handing her history on the revision path (relies on the Step-15 pending-draft context + her not needing the full thread); OR
- **(b)** keep giving her the history but **label it clearly as read-only reference** — *"here's the thread for your context; do NOT include it in your draft — the runtime attaches it"* — via a small revision-prompt change + a `core.md` clarification.

**AC2a's lean:** (b) — cheap, preserves the revision-context the QC fix added, and matches core.md's existing framing. But it's genuinely a call to settle, not a foregone conclusion. **Once the direction is chosen, the `core.md` (and possibly revision-prompt) wording is the beat.**
