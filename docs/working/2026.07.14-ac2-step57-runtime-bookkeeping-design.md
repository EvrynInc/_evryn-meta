## 1. What this is (plain)

Today, when Evryn triages a forwarded email, **she does the record-keeping herself**: she looks up whether she's seen the sender before (a tool call), sometimes creates their user record (a tool call), writes statuses to the database with a raw generic write tool (`supabase_upsert`), and each of those round-trips re-sends her entire ~250k-token context to the model. A routine "pass" costs ~15 turns / ~$1.92 cold. The measured breakdown (cost-levers §4) shows most of those turns are **deterministic bookkeeping the runtime could do for free**.

Step 57 inverts it: **the runtime pre-loads everything she needs, and persists everything she decides. Evryn does only judgment, and returns her verdict through one structured tool call.** A pass drops to ~2–4 turns; a gold to ~5–6. As a by-product, her raw database write surface is **removed entirely** — the write-side sibling of AC4's read-lock, and ADR-033's "structural enforcement at tier 5" done properly.

## 2. The tool surface after Step 57

**Removed:**
- `supabase_upsert` — gone entirely. Every status/lifecycle write it carried moves into typed verdict tools or the runtime. This also deletes the Step-18b interim (`decideUpsertRouting`) and the note-turn upsert collision guard (`isNoteTurnGuardedUpsert`) — the vector no longer exists. Rare ad-hoc write needs (an operator asking for a one-off correction) surface to the Operator, who has CC/Supabase; if a recurring need emerges, we build a typed tool for it.
- `record_pass` (the tool) — absorbed into `record_verdict` below. The `recordPass` *function* (stamp logic, idempotency, null-contact handling) survives as the pass branch of the verdict hook.

**New — `record_verdict`** `({emailmgr_item_id, verdict, reasoning?, contact_user_id?})`. One tool, one call, terminal. The verdict enum names Evryn's real disposition space (this is what resolves Justin's sender_type-vs-status divergence case — see §4):

| Verdict | Meaning | `sender_type` | `status` | `triage_result` | User record | Forward persisted (54b) | Pass-stamp |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| `pass` | real person, not a fit here | `lead` | `passed` | `pass` | ✅ create/find | ✅ | ✅ |
| `ignore` | junk — no person behind it | `ignore` | `ignored` | — | ❌ none | ❌ | ❌ |
| `bad_actor` | real person, acting deceptively | `bad_actor` | `bad_actor` | — | ✅ create + `users.status=bad_actor` | ✅ | ❌ |
| `handled_by_gatekeeper` | real person; gatekeeper says "already dealt with it / ignore it" | `lead` | `ignored` | — | ✅ create/find | ✅ | ❌ |
| `not_a_candidate` | the forward isn't triage at all (a message to Evryn, context, info she asked for) | untouched | `ignored` | — | ❌ | ❌ | ❌ |
| `escalate` | precondition failure / ambiguity — operator needs eyes | untouched | `escalated` | — | ❌ (deferred to resolution) | ❌ | ❌ |

- `reasoning` → `triage_reasoning`; the tight-by-default discipline carries over verbatim from `record_pass` (slam-dunk = none; a non-obvious downgrade = a few lines).
- `contact_user_id` is consulted **only** when the item's own sender resolution fails (parse-hiccup backstop, 54b brief §4) and is validated (`validateUserIsValidTarget`) — never trusted raw (Finding-16 fabrication defense carries over).
- Gold/edge do **not** go through `record_verdict` — the draft IS the verdict act:

**Changed — `submit_draft`**: when `classification ∈ {gold, edge}` on a forward-triage item, `submitDraftForApproval` additionally runs the verdict hook — writes `triage_result = gold|edge` + `sender_type = lead`, find-or-creates the contact, backfills the item FK, persists the forward (54b). This **canonicalizes `triage_result` for all three verdicts** (the ADR-018 amendment; closes the gap the Haiku shadow-trial's `deriveOpusVerdict` reads around — that pure fn needs *zero change*: its branch (i) reads `triage_result` first, so post-③ it simply always hits). Written at *submit*, not approval — the verdict is Evryn's triage-time judgment; Justin's approval gates the *send*. `reply`-classification drafts never touch the verdict hook.

**New — `record_outcome`** `({emailmgr_item_id, outcome: matched|passed|no_gk_response, note?})`. The post-delivery lifecycle closer (gatekeeper feedback / follow-up timeout) — replaces the raw-upsert instructions in the `checkFollowUps` prompt and feedback handling. Validates the item is in a delivered/follow-up state (which also structurally protects queued items — no note-turn guard needed for it). **`matched` carries the 54b #2 re-attribution**: atomically repoint the preserved forward message's `recipient_id` → `metadata.intended_for` (contact↔Evryn becomes contact↔gatekeeper — the forward enters Mark's history *at match, never before*). Re-attribute first, then status-flip (both idempotent, so a mid-sequence crash retries cleanly).

**Unchanged:** constrained `supabase_read`, `create_user`, `find_user_by_name`, `append_pending_note`, `append_cross_user_note`, `notify_slack`, `register_gatekeeper_address`, `read_identity_module`, the operator conditionals, `resolve_noted_forward`.

**Note-turn residual — closed structurally:** in a note-turn, `record_verdict` gets the same pin guard `submit_draft` has (refuse when the arg item == the note-turn's pinned item; `resolve_noted_forward` is the only door). With `supabase_upsert` deleted and `record_pass` absorbed, the ② accepted-residual's whole vector class is gone.

**Reply-item doctrine (generalizes the ② note-turn rule):** *a triage item's status is owned by the verdict tools; Evryn's replies ride their own placeholder items.* A `reply`-classification `submit_draft` against a **pre-verdict** forward item routes to a placeholder (mirror of `resolveNoteTurnSubmitItemId`, now uniform); post-delivery follow-ups attach as today (the legitimate `delivered → pending_approval → delivered` cycle).

## 3. The verdict hook (runtime) + pre-loading

**New module `src/triage/verdict.ts`** — `applyVerdict(item, disposition, opts, deps?)`, the single deterministic writer. Callers: the `record_verdict` tool, `submitDraftForApproval` (gold/edge), the Haiku screen's active-pass/active-ignore branches (replacing their direct `recordPass`/`recordIgnore` calls — one unified path), and `waveOffNotedForward` (maps to `handled_by_gatekeeper`). Injectable-deps + pure-helper pattern per codebase convention. Idempotent throughout (already-terminal → benign no-op, extending `isAlreadyPassed`; the 54b message write checks `metadata.emailmgr_item_id` before insert). All transitions via `updateEmailmgrItem` (lifecycle-appended).

**Contact resolution order** (real-person verdicts): item FK if set → find-or-create by normalized `original_from` email (referrer = the gatekeeper) → validated `contact_user_id` arg → null (transition still lands; stamp/persist skipped + `notifyDev`, per existing `recordPass` posture).

**Pre-loading (the turn-killer):** `runForwardTriage` adds two runtime-fetched blocks to the prompt (per-item variable data — prompt, never systemPrompt; the untrusted fence stays email-content-only since these are our own DB records):
1. **Prior-triage history** — `emailmgr_items` by `original_from_user_id` (FK) with fallback to `original_from` email match, excluding the current item: date · verdict · status · `triage_reasoning`, compact. Replaces triage.md's Phase-1 lookup procedure (the judgment guidance — resubmit-gaming, prior-gold follow-up, prior-bad_actor auto-flag — stays, now applied to handed context).
2. **Contact context** — if a user record resolves by email: their story + pending_notes, compact. Also powers the shared-email safety beat: *if the pre-loaded contact reads as a different person than this email's sender, `record_verdict(escalate)` — never merge.*

Cost check: the blocks add ~1–3k prompt tokens (~$0.01) vs. removing 2–4 full-context turns (~$0.06+ each) — clearly net-positive.

**M1/halt interplay (verified against the loaded runtime):** a breaker trip short-circuits at the top of `runEvrynQuery` *before* any tool call → item parks with no partial hook; a trip after `record_verdict` finds the item already terminal → `parkInFlightItemForHalt` no-ops (it only parks `processing`). The B2c backstop (`ensureItemTransitioned`) still catches a query that ends with no verdict call. No M1 changes needed.

## 4. User-creation-at-verdict (Justin ballot item 1)

Today `processForward` reifies **every** parseable sender as a `lead` user record the moment the forward arrives (ADR-036) — so spam senders get durable user rows. **AC2 recommends moving forward-path user-creation to verdict-time, creating no record for `ignore`** (Justin's stated instinct, 2026-07-07):

- The §2 matrix already IS this design — one verdict hook does create + FK-backfill + persist + status in a single deterministic act. It's *more* deterministic than today (the FK no longer depends on a parse racing a Supabase blip at ingest).
- **The "have I seen them before?" wrinkle resolves by email:** pre-verdict, the item has no FK, so the prior-history pre-load falls back to the `original_from` email match (past real-person verdicts *did* set FKs; junk history stays queryable by email on old items within the 6-month TTL). Beyond the TTL a junk re-sender is simply re-screened fresh — the Haiku bouncer makes that cheap.
- **Justin's illustrative case gets a first-class answer:** Mark forwards *"I already took care of this — ignore it."* That's `handled_by_gatekeeper`: the **item** closes (`status=ignored`) but the **person** is real (`sender_type=lead`, record created, forward preserved for the v0.3 pool). The two axes (ADR-018's `sender_type` = is-there-a-person; `status` = the item's disposition) legitimately diverge — the doctrine goes in the ADR-018 amendment.
- Direct messages (`processDirect`) keep arrival-time creation — a real person initiating contact. Forwards only.
- ADR-036 amendment required (reification moves from forward-time to verdict-time). If Justin instead keeps forward-time creation, the design degrades gracefully (the hook's resolution order just always finds the FK; junk rows persist as today) — the two questions are separable.

## 5. What does NOT change

Slack-operator pathway · approval-flow mechanics · quiet hours · M1 breaker/halt semantics + park targets · Clustering drain shape (`triageQueuedItem`, `tallyItemOutcome` buckets) · the Haiku screen itself (only its active branches route through the unified hook) · `processDirect` arrival-time behavior · `getRecentMessages` isolation invariant (contact↔Evryn rows enter Mark's history only via the deliberate `matched` re-attribution) · `deriveOpusVerdict` (pure fn untouched; branch (iii) becomes legacy) · **zero schema migration** (the `triage_result` CHECK already allows gold/pass/edge; `messages.metadata` already exists) — the deploy carries no psql step.
