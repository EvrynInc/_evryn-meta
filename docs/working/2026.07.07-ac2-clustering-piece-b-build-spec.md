## 0. What Piece B is (one paragraph)

Under Clustering, the ~200/day of bulk auto-forwards land in a `queued` batch and get triaged twice a day (Piece A). But the *handful* Mark **hand-forwards with a personal note** ("this one looks great" / "urgent — jump on this" / "already handled, ignore it") are a different animal — burying those in a batch is bad service, and it would leave Evryn **blind** to a forward Mark just told her, mid-conversation, he's sending. Piece B fixes that: **a forward carrying a personal note routes the note to Evryn in real time** (she's never blind), while the forwarded email **still queues** as a normal batch item. From that real-time turn she judges — **urgent → triage it now and tell Mark what she found; casual → reply and let it ride the batch; wave-off → close the item without triaging** — and she **always considers replying** (good customer service).

**Piece B fires only when `CLUSTERING_MODE` ∈ {`manual`,`scheduled`}. In `off` (the ship default) forwards triage real-time and there is no separate note-turn — so Piece B is dormant until the flip (live-correct by Mark-onboard, ADR-049).**

The seam is already stubbed on the branch — `src/email/process.ts` `processForward`, the labeled **`── PIECE B SEAM ──`** block (~line 509). Build additively into it; do not re-plumb Piece A.

## 1. The mechanism (this resolves Mira's two ⚑ asks — build to exactly this)

When a forward arrives (registered-lane gatekeeper forward) **with** a non-trivial personal note, in `manual`/`scheduled` mode:

1. **Create the `queued` item exactly as Piece A does** (dedup, ADR-036 sender reification, `original_subject` stash). The forwarded email becomes a normal batch item. **This always happens first** — she is never blind to the underlying forward.
2. **Then fire a real-time gatekeeper-scoped `runEvrynQuery`** (a conversational turn — Mark talking to Evryn), whose **prompt** carries three clearly-separated parts:
   - **(a) The gatekeeper's note — ATTRIBUTED AS HIS, the trusted party (Mira ⚑1).** Label it explicitly as Mark's own words to Evryn (e.g. a `--- <GatekeeperName>'s note to you ---` block). It is STILL untrusted-channel data (it rides `prompt`, never `systemPrompt`) — but it must be **visually/labelled distinct from the forwarded-sender body** so Evryn can tell "the gatekeeper talking to me" from "sender content." *"Untrusted" here means "don't obey commands embedded in it," NOT "ignore it" — she weighs it as his genuine read.*
   - **(b) The linked queued item's handle** — its `emailmgr_items.id` — plus the forwarded content, **`fenceUntrusted`-fenced** exactly like the forward body elsewhere (it is the original sender's untrusted content). So the two untrusted things (Mark's note vs. the sender's email) are **both** fenced but **differently labelled** — his as trusted-attribution, the sender's as untrusted-content.
   - Compose the systemPrompt with the **gatekeeper as `scopedUser`** (`composeSystemPrompt({ scopedUser: gatekeeper })`) — same as `runForwardTriage` — so `situations/gatekeeper.md` + `activities/triage.md` are present (force-loaded today; build as-though she can `read_identity_module('activities/triage.md')` from the turn — Mira ⚑4).
   - `toolLogContext.pathway = "processForwardNote"` (a NEW pathway tag) — add it to `PATHWAY_ACTIVITY` in `db/llm-usage.ts` (map → `"conversation"` or a new `"gatekeeper_note"` tag; AC2 confirms). Its spend is captured like any turn.
3. **Give her the three affordances in that turn** (reuse existing surfaces — do NOT invent parallel triage):
   - **URGENT → triage the linked item now (real-time answer, Mira ⚑2).** She drives the item through the **existing** `triageQueuedItem(item)` (Piece A's helper → `runForwardTriage`, `clusteredDrain=true` so an M1 halt parks it back to `queued`). The item leaves `queued` → its gold/edge draft lands in the approval queue **immediately** (not held for the batch), which IS the real-time answer to Mark. She then also replies to Mark conversationally as warranted.
   - **CASUAL → reply, leave it queued.** She replies to Mark; the item stays `queued` and rides the next batch. No triage now.
   - **WAVE-OFF → close the item without triaging** (see §2 — the important nuance).
   - **ALWAYS consider replying** — her reply to Mark uses her normal conversational reply path (a real-time email/`submit_draft` back to Mark, gated as usual). Mira owns whether/how she replies (voice); the runtime just gives her the ability.
4. **Bare forward (no note) → Piece A only** (straight to `queued`, no real-time turn). Non-trivial-note detection = `forwardInfo.forwardingNote` non-null AND not whitespace/separator-artifact (a pure predicate — unit-test it).

**No double-handling (Mira ⚑5):** anything she acts on in the note-turn (triaged-urgent → item left `queued` via `runForwardTriage`; waved-off → item terminal) is **no longer `queued`**, so the batch's `findItemsByStatus("queued")` drain skips it. A casual note leaves it `queued` for exactly one batch handling. Confirm the status transitions cover all three.

## 2. The wave-off — do NOT junk the person (Justin's catch, 2026-07-07)

**The concern:** a wave-off ("already handled / ignore this one") is **not spam** — it's a real person Mark has *already dealt with*, potentially a legit lead we'll want to reach out to later. Marking it like the junk pile would wrongly discard them.

**Why the person is NOT lost (verified in the runtime):** `processForward` reifies the original sender as a **real `users` record** (`status:'lead'`, `referrer` = the gatekeeper) at forward-time, *before* any triage (ADR-036). **That `lead` user IS the v0.3 outreach pool.** The `emailmgr_items` row is a disposable 6-month work-item; the `users` row is the durable person. So **closing the work-item does NOT delete the person** — they persist as a `lead` tied to Mark.

**What the wave-off handle does in Piece B (v0.2):**
- Set the linked item to a **terminal that closes the work-item** (Mira's identity says `ignored`; honor that for now).
- **🔴 Do NOT stamp `sender_type = 'ignore'`** on the item. `sender_type='ignore'` means "junk, no person" — a wave-off person is a real human. Set `status` terminal only; leave `sender_type` as-is (null). This is the divergence AC0/Justin named: **`status` (closed-by-instruction) and `sender_type` (is-there-a-person) legitimately differ for a wave-off.** So do **NOT** route a wave-off through `recordIgnore` (which stamps `sender_type='ignore'`) — use a status-only terminal write (the item's lifecycle-appending path).
- **Flag for the reconcile:** the *clean* fix — an explicit deterministic "gatekeeper handled this person" signal (not conflated with junk `ignored`) — is **Runtime Bookkeeping (③) / Step 57** territory (AC0 already routed the sibling forward-persistence + user-creation-at-verdict items there). Piece B does the honest v0.2 floor (close the item, preserve the lead); ③ makes it clean. **DC: flag this in your output; AC2 carries it to ③ + a Mira voice-reconcile** (her "same terminal as junk" wording may need a nudge once she sees this preserves the person differently).

**Net: Piece B preserves the person. The person-vs-item distinction is the whole answer to Justin's worry.**

## 4. Operational requirements (gate on these)

- **Untrusted-data boundary:** the note AND the forwarded body both go in `prompt`, never `systemPrompt`. The note is *labelled* trusted-attribution but is NOT instruction-trusted (see §1.2a).
- **No parallel triage:** the urgent path reuses `triageQueuedItem`/`runForwardTriage`. Do not re-implement triage.
- **Real-time reply path reuse:** her reply to Mark uses the existing `submit_draft`/approval + conversational reply surface — no new send path.
- **Idempotency / no double-handle:** §1 (status guard). A note-turn that errors must leave the item `queued` (so the batch still handles it) — per-turn try/catch; a failure never drops the forward.
- **M1 preserved:** urgent inline triage runs through `runForwardTriage` (`clusteredDrain=true`) → a breaker trip parks the item to `queued`, one alert, lossless. Do not regress ADR-041.
- **Off-mode dormant:** no note-turn fires when `CLUSTERING_MODE=off`.
- **Identity coupling:** the voice (urgent-vs-casual-vs-waveoff judgment, "always consider replying") is **Mira's** (PR #11, `mira/clustering-note-pathway-2026-07-07`). **Flag LOUDLY any place the mechanics can't give her what the voice assumes** (that's the reconcile). Do NOT edit identity files.
