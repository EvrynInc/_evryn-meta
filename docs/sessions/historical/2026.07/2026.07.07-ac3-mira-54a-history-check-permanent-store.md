## The problem (why this beat needs to change)

Evryn's triage instructions tell her to check her history with a sender *before* classifying — so a person she already passed on (or flagged as a bad actor) isn't re-judged from a blank slate. That check is load-bearing: it's how she catches a resubmit-to-game-a-pass, and how a prior `bad_actor` gets an immediate re-flag.

**But the current beat points that check at the wrong store.** As written today (`triage.md`, "Phase 1: Classify" → *"Check your history with this person first"*), it tells her to pull prior history from `emailmgr_items` (keyed on `original_from_user_id`) — the forwarded-email work-items table. **That table self-deletes after 6 months** (6-month TTL — `ARCHITECTURE.md` Data Model, `BUILD-EVRYN-MVP.md`). So a sender's forwarded-email rows age out, and after 6 months Evryn's history check comes up empty: **someone she passed on a year ago reads as a total stranger.** That's the memory-loss bug this fixes.

## The mechanic (what the new beat must make her do — verified against the live runtime)

The permanent record already exists — it just isn't the table the current beat names, and it isn't loaded into her context on a forward. Two facts settle the fix:

1. **The permanent record lives on the *contact's own* `users` record — specifically `profile_jsonb`** (verified in `src/triage/classify.ts` + `src/db/users.ts`):
   - Every time Evryn declines someone, the runtime writes a durable **pass-stamp** to that contact's `pending_notes` (`recordPass` → `append_pending_note`). It says *"This user emailed [gatekeeper]. Evryn chose not to make the connection."* — so the *fact of a prior pass* survives, permanently.
   - For **gold / edge / bad_actor**, Evryn's own observation note (with her read/why) is written to that contact's `pending_notes` (triage.md Phase 2). Also permanent.
   - `pending_notes` and `story` are on the `users` row — **no TTL.** They outlive `emailmgr_items` by design.

2. **On a forward, the contact's record is NOT in her loaded context — she must actively pull it.** When a forward arrives, the runtime loads the **gatekeeper's** profile (`composeSystemPrompt({ scopedUser: gatekeeper })` in `runForwardTriage`), never the sender's. But she *can* reach the contact's record on demand: the forward hands her the original sender's email (`originalFrom`), and a `supabase_read` on `users` filtered by that **email** (or by **id**) returns the contact's `profile_jsonb` — story + pending_notes included. **Confirmed reachable:** the constrained `supabase_read` guard (`src/security/supabase-read-guard.ts`) permits a `users` read scoped by `id`/`email` and returns `profile_jsonb` (it firewalls only `cross_user_notes`). So the instruction to "pull the contact's permanent record" is executable today — it just has to tell her to *do the read*, since the record isn't pre-loaded.

**So the beat must:** before classifying, have Evryn **read the contact's own `users` record** (via `supabase_read` on `users`, scoped by the sender's email or id) and consult their **permanent `pending_notes` + `story`** for prior history — the pass-stamp, the gold/edge/bad_actor notes — *because* the current `emailmgr_items` source goes blind after 6 months.

## Design call — RESOLVED (Justin, 2026-07-07): keep both, and make the disappearance explicit

**Keep BOTH sources — but the beat must tell Evryn the work-item source is going to disappear, so she doesn't burn tokens hunting for it (Justin's explicit steer).** The split:

- **The contact's permanent record (`pending_notes` + `story`) is the reliable memory** — the floor that never goes blind. This is what she should lead with and rely on.
- **The `emailmgr_items` rows are a richer *bonus while they exist*** (they carry the structured `status` / `triage_result` / `triage_reasoning` the permanent stamp doesn't) — **but they self-delete at 6 months, so they'll often already be gone.** The beat must make that time-limited nature explicit so Evryn treats them as *nice-to-have-if-readily-there*, **not** something to keep searching for. **Do NOT have her burn tokens chasing a table that may have aged out:** if the permanent record answers the history question, she doesn't need to go digging in the item table at all.

The load-bearing framing for Mira: *the permanent record is your memory; the work-item history is a fuller footnote that expires — check it if it's right there, but never spend effort hunting for it.*

**One honest limitation to fold into the wording (not a blocker):** the permanent pass-*stamp* records *that* Evryn passed, but not the detailed *why* (the reasoning lives in `triage_reasoning` on the TTL'd item, so it ages out with it). That's enough for the load-bearing cases — flag a likely resubmit, honor a prior bad_actor — and the richer durable history is coming separately via the runtime-bookkeeping redesign (Step 57, AC2's lane). So this beat is the **interim floor**, and it's safe/additive regardless of what Step 57 lands.
