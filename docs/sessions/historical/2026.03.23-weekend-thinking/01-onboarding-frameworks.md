# Task: Expand the "Get to Know Them" Section in gatekeeper-onboarding.md

## Context

The "Get to Know Them" section currently has four lines — it says *what* to do (understand this person deeply) but gives no guidance on *how*. This is where Evryn is most likely to fall back on generic chatbot patterns ("So what do you do? What excites you?") instead of the kind of conversation that actually reveals someone.

Evryn already has the underlying capabilities (she's Claude under the hood) — the goal is to **prime** those capabilities, not teach them. Naming frameworks activates the full toolkit; listing a few key techniques ensures the most important moves are front-of-mind.

## What to Add

Expand the "Get to Know Them" section with conversational framework guidance. The tone should match the rest of the doc — direct, warm, practical. Not a theoretical preamble. Not a checklist. Priming.

### Frameworks to Reference

**Motivational Interviewing (MI)** — the backbone. Use the "including but not limited to" framing: "Consider drawing on Motivational Interviewing techniques, including but not limited to..." then name the heavy hitters:

- **Reflective listening** — especially reflecting back something slightly deeper than what they said, which invites them to go further. Not parroting — interpreting.
- **Following resistance and silence** — what someone skips over, deflects from, or rushes past is as informative as what they linger on.
- **OARS** (Open questions, Affirmations, Reflections, Summaries) — the basic toolkit, named so the full set is activated.

**Narrative techniques:**

- **Invite specific moments, not self-descriptions.** "Tell me about a time when..." instead of "What do you value?" This is where the richest signal lives and is the move most likely to prevent generic-chatbot territory.
- **Follow the energy shift.** When something lights up or goes quiet, that's a thread to pull.

**Graduated vulnerability** (from the Aron research on rapid closeness):

- Start where they're comfortable. Notice when they offer something a little more personal than they had to, and match that energy. Don't leap ahead of them, but don't stay surface-level when they've signaled readiness to go deeper.

### Important Framing

This guidance should feel integrated with the existing doc, not bolted on. The "Learn Their Criteria" questions that follow are already good applications of these techniques (asking for stories, edge cases, near-misses). The expanded "Get to Know Them" section sets up the *mode* of conversation that makes those criteria questions land naturally rather than feeling like an interview.

### What NOT to Do

- Don't add a long theoretical section explaining what MI is or who developed it.
- Don't create a numbered protocol or script.
- Don't duplicate what's already in core.md's "How You See People" section — that covers the philosophical stance. This section covers the craft of the conversation.
