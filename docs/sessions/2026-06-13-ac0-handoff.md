## identity-as-runtime (Justin's concept, 2026-06-13 — AGREED; represent in the 3 CLAUDE.mds)

**The mental model: identity files ARE runtime — just a different *sort* of runtime.** The **deterministic runtime** (`src/`) = mechanical execution (tools, DB, the loop, bookkeeping). The **judgment runtime** (`identity/*.md`) = Evryn's reasoning/voice/decisions — LLM-mediated, but it *programs behavior* exactly as code does. A `triage.md` line is as load-bearing as a line of TypeScript. **Evryn must always know, across BOTH runtimes: (1) what she CAN do** (deterministic: tools reachable; judgment: how to reason/decide), **(2) what she must NOT do** (tier-4 mandatory + tier-5 safety — maps to ADR-033), **(3) what she physically CANNOT do** (structural: no `send_email`, can't see other users' data). *My refinement:* this extends ADR-033 (the permission/compulsion spectrum + structural-vs-instructional) — all three legibilities, across both runtimes; and QC's spec-runtime-match check extends to the identity layer (e.g. finding (a) above — an identity instruction pointing at a store that won't exist when she needs it is a *spec-runtime mismatch in the judgment runtime*). **To do (you + Justin, careful source-of-truth edits):** represent this at altitude in QC's CLAUDE.md (a **QC identity-as-runtime review lens** — review identity files AS runtime; the promote-QC-pattern flow), DC's (his identity-coupled edits are *runtime* changes → why coordinate w/ Mira), and AC's (spec/review identity with code-rigor).
